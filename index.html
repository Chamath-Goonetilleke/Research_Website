Project Scope


Literature Survey

Traffic congestion presents a significant challenge in various countries, including Sri Lanka. The swift urbanization and increased use of motor vehicles in Sri Lanka have contributed to a rise in traffic jams, causing adverse effects on the environment, economy, and society. According to a report published by the World Bank, Colombo, the largest city in Sri Lanka, experiences an annual economic loss of approximately US$2 billion due to traffic congestion [1].

Adaptive traffic control systems collect traffic information at intersections using various sensing technologies. These include video cameras, LiDAR, radar, and inductive loops. [2] These sensors enable getting data about current traffic information. Adaptive traffic control systems utilize fuzzy logic, genetic algorithms, and reinforcement learning to optimize traffic flow and reduce congestion.

New techniques like YOLO and Fast-RCNN are being used to detect emergency vehicles in real time. These methods use deep learning and image processing to identify vehicles and siren lights accurately. Also, models like 1D CNN and LSTM are being developed to detect critical siren sounds. While there are limitations to these technologies, they are helping to create a more reliable system for detecting emergency vehicles and making the roads safer.

The accident severity detection component is based on the YOLOv8 (You Look Only Once Version 8) architecture, a deep learning model designed for image recognition and classification tasks. YOLOv8 is based on cutting-edge advances in deep learning and computer vision, providing excellent speed and accuracy. In the context of accident severity detection, this model can automatically extract meaningful features from accident images to predict severity levels.

This research uses image subtraction technology to analyze sequential video frames to identify traffic offenses. This technique isolates moving objects, and potential violations are shown. Additionally, the study uses YOLO (You Only Look Once) to identify motorcyclists who are not wearing helmets instantly. By accurately identifying situations where riders violate helmet laws, YOLO's sophisticated object detection abilities enhance road safety measures.

Research Gap

Following areas are the research gaps found in most of the recent researches.

Traffic Light Optimization

Smart traffic systems have been implemented in many countries to improve traffic flow. However, there have been relatively few research studies that consider all these parameters, including the number of vehicles, types of vehicles on the road, as well as the average waiting times for vehicles.

Emergency Vehicle Prioritization

A research gap exists in developing an advanced emergency vehicle prioritization system that utilizes innovative methods for visual and sound verification, ensuring accurate and prompt detection of emergency vehicles in congested traffic conditions.

Accident Severity Detection

There are CCTV cameras, they are primarily used for monitoring traffic flow and not for detecting accidents. A research gap exists in developing an efficient and accurate real-time detection especially focusing on identifying accident severity levels

Traffic Violation Detection

There is a research gap in developing a comprehensive traffic violation monitoring system that utilizes advanced technologies, identify and penalize various types of traffic violations. This system should focus on enhancing road safety by deterring and penalizing violators



Research Problem & Solution

Proposed Problem


How can advanced technologies optimize intersection traffic, prioritize emergency vehicles, detect accidents, alert services, and prevent violations for enhanced road safety?

Traffic congestion has been a growing problem affecting various industries worldwide for many years. Because most of Sri Lanka's traffic lights are pre-timed, the times for green and red are static, which causes congestion on most roads. Here a novel traffic algorithm will be used to optimize the traffic. By utilizing real-time data from vehicle detection and tracking, the algorithm determines optimal traffic signal timings, prioritizing the movement of vehicles and minimizing traffic congestion.


Proposed Solution

The study investigates the novel application of deep neural networks and image-processing technologies for intersection traffic control and emergency vehicle prioritizing, detecting violations of traffic laws and accident severity. The method combines Q-Learning and an optimization algorithm for effective traffic flow optimization. The comprehensive system includes a user-friendly web based monitoring program that focuses on isolated intersection traffic flow and is accessible to authorized parties like the police department. The system uses YOLO, TensorFlow, and Convolutional

Research Objectives


Traffic Signal Optimization

The First objective is developing an intelligent traffic light system is a multifaceted research endeavor driven by several interconnected objectives, each aimed at addressing the pressing challenges of traffic congestion, energy conservation, and urban mobility. At its core, this project seeks to revolutionize the way traffic is managed in urban environments, fostering efficiency, sustainability, and improved quality of life.

Emergency Vehicle Prioritization

The second objective is to enhance the priority given to emergency vehicles, including ambulances, police vehicles, and firetrucks, within an intersection's traffic light system. The system's procedure involves identifying an emergency vehicle and verifying the presence of an emergency situation within that vehicle by inspecting both the siren lights and siren sound. If either the siren lights or siren sound is activated, the system deduces the existence of an emergency. In the event of an emergency vehicle with a confirmed emergency situation, the system proceeds to halt traffic in other lanes and facilitate the unobstructed passage of the emergency vehicle.

Accident Detection

The third objective is to implement a comprehensive accident severity detection system that instantly notifies emergency services when a high-severity accident happens. This system intends to improve traffic safety by delivering early and accurate assessments of accident severity and aiding speedy rescue operations by leveraging advanced technologies such as deep learning and computer vision.

Traffic Violations Detection

The fourth objective is ensuring road safety and enforcing traffic regulations. These systems utilize advanced technologies such as computer vision and machine learning algorithms to identify and monitor various violations, including helmet detection, speed tracking, and wrong-way driving.
